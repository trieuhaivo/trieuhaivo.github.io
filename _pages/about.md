I am a **PhD Student in Computer Science** at [**Florida International University**](https://www.fiu.edu/) in Miami, Florida, conducting research at the intersection of **deep learning** and **environmental monitoring**. My doctoral work is advised by [**Prof. Leonardo Bobadilla**](https://www.cis.fiu.edu/faculty-staff/leonardo-bobadilla/) (Associate Professor, Knight Foundation School of Computing and Information Sciences, Florida International University) and [**Prof. Cuong V. Nguyen**](https://www.durham.ac.uk/staff/viet-c-nguyen/) (Assistant Professor, Department of Mathematical Sciences, Durham University, United Kingdom).

I develop **deep-learning methods for environmental time series**, with applications to **water-quality monitoring** and **climate observation** — infrastructure that U.S. agencies, water utilities, and federal Earth-system research increasingly depend on. I published peer-reviewed work at the **[ICLR 2025 Workshop on Tackling Climate Change with Machine Learning](https://www.climatechange.ai/events/iclr2025)** (the dedicated climate-AI workshop co-located with the **[International Conference on Learning Representations](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2025)**) and submitted my current research to **[ACM SIGKDD 2026](https://kdd2026.kdd.org/)**, currently under peer review.

Two NSF-funded research centers at Florida International University fund my doctoral research. The **[NSF CREST Center for Aquatic Chemistry and Environment (CACHE)](https://crestcache.fiu.edu/)** anchors the aquatic-environment domain — water-quality monitoring, lake stratification, coastal chemistry — and tracks my annual research outputs in NSF program reports. The **[NSF AI Institute for Research on Trustworthy AI in Weather, Climate, and Coastal Oceanography (AI2ES)](https://www.ai2es.org/)** via the **FIU–AI2ES ExpandAI** partnership anchors the trustworthy-AI methodology behind Earth-system and climate-resilience applications. I co-presented *"Learning and Planning for Water Quality Monitoring"* with Prof. Bobadilla at the AI2ES collaboration meeting at FIU in June 2024 and report progress at monthly AI2ES coordination meetings. I am also a graduate researcher in Prof. Bobadilla's [**Motion, Robotics and Automation Lab**](https://robotics.cs.fiu.edu/robotics/people/) at the Knight Foundation School of Computing and Information Sciences.

I hold an **M.S. in Artificial Intelligence** from [**Northeastern University**](https://www.northeastern.edu/) (2023, GPA 3.91 / 4.00) and a **B.S. in Computer Science** (Advanced Program) from [**Vietnam National University — University of Science**](https://en.hcmus.edu.vn/) (2018, GPA 3.65 / 4.00), where I received the *Rector's Merit Award and Medal* for top academic standing. Prior to my PhD, I worked as an **AI Engineer** at [**Breathing.ai**](https://www.breathing.ai/) (New York; remote), deploying real-time physiological-sensing systems on Amazon Web Services. I passed the **PhD Qualifying Examination** at Florida International University in **April 2026**.

<div class="research-focus">
<strong>Research Focus:</strong> I develop <strong>deep-learning methods for environmental time series</strong> — specifically, <strong>structured attention architectures</strong> that encode the physical structure of stratified aquatic systems along three axes: depth, feature, and time. My research uses the climate and water-monitoring data that U.S. federal programs depend on — including the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's Safe Drinking Water Act monitoring, the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration's coastal-resilience initiatives, and the U.S. Geological Survey's National Water Information System — covering lake-temperature records, multi-depth water-quality measurements, and long-horizon environmental forecasts. <strong>Depth-wise Multivariate Imputation (DMI)</strong> gives depth its own attention axis rather than collapsing it into the feature dimension, and outperforms standard multivariate-imputation baselines on the LakeBeD-US and Miami-WQ benchmarks. Immediate applications include drinking-water reservoirs, coastal-resilience monitoring, and the climate-change-attribution datasets underlying U.S. federal Earth-system research.
</div>

**Research interests:** time-series imputation and forecasting · structured attention architectures · diffusion and generative models for scientific data · physics-informed machine learning · foundation models for environmental applications.
